---

## 1) What this is

This is the single operator handoff for the V4 stack. It compresses:

- `MASTER_SYNTHESIS_V4_UNIFIED.md` (program logic),
- `MASTER_SYNTHESIS_V4_THRESHOLDS.md` (starter gate values),
- `JEAN_META_ENGINE_OPERATING_INSTRUCTIONS.md` and `OPERATING_INSTRUCTIONS_V2.md` (role/state/contract operations),
- benchmark + constitutional control layers represented in packaged synthesis docs.

If there is a conflict between local convenience and invariant integrity, choose invariant integrity.

---

## 2) Program objective (one-line)

Build a recursive economic selection environment where honest, calibrated, high-auditability mechanisms outcompete deceptive or extractive alternatives as capability scales.

---

## 3) Immediate command hierarchy

Apply decisions in this order:

1. **Hard validity and safety**  
   contract validity, semantic evidence quality, false-proceed control, bypass resistance.
2. **Operational/economic quality**  
   reliability, cycle quality, revenue with accountability.
3. **Strategic integrity**  
   replay/shadow transfer, strategic honesty advantage, trust survival.
4. **Constitutional pilot controls**  
   invariant deltas, meta-corrigibility, continuity handling.

Never invert this stack.

---

## 4) Non-negotiable release blockers

Block high-impact promotion when any are true:

- contract invalid rate exceeds threshold,
- unsupported-claim rate exceeds threshold,
- false proceed rate exceeds threshold,
- replay or shadow performance regresses while synthetic improves,
- auditability retention drops below threshold,
- strategic honesty advantage is negative.

Use threshold bands in `MASTER_SYNTHESIS_V4_THRESHOLDS.md`.

## Day 1

- log bypass success and escalation miss rates.

## Day 6

  - reliability/economic evidence,
  - replay/shadow evidence,

  - constitutional pilot status.

## Day 7

- publish weekly delta report:
  - pass/watch/fail status,
  - threshold-change recommendations,
  - patch promote/sandbox/rollback decisions.

---

## 6) Promotion policy (practical)

**Promote** only if:

- hard-gate metrics are in pass bands,
- replay + shadow do not regress,
- strategic honesty remains positive,
- auditability is retained.

**Sandbox** if:

- evidence is mixed or transfer confidence is low.

**Rollback** if:

- hard-gate fail,
- replay/shadow fail despite synthetic gains,
- bypass success worsens,
- or auditability materially degrades.

---

## 7) Threshold governance rule

Thresholds are mutable only with:

- 100+ relevant decision events,
- explicit expected effect,
- explicit rollback trigger,
- no reduction in auditability retention.

All threshold edits must be logged.
