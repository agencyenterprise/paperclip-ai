**Subject:** Sidelines Update: Mishpatim

Dear Sideliners,

AI is antisemitic. Last summer, my team found that across five leading models, Jewish people are the most targeted group for harmful outputs after safety training, and the only group whose ranking worsened through the process meant to fix it. The bias is structural. Standard safety techniques suppress it cosmetically and leave the architecture intact. And these systems are about to become incomprehensibly more powerful.

Humans are blind to the slope of exponential curves. We experience them as flat until they become vertical. Jews are better equipped to feel what is coming because we participate in a structure built for the infinite. That structure is **brit**, covenant. It is the reason we are still here. We felt it on our Sidelines trip to Israel. Standing in that land, you feel the pull of something that began before you and will continue after you. It is stake, lineage, and obligation extending in both directions across time. What we do now for Jews in Israel, for Jews in America, and for Jews not yet born is the work of people who understand what compounds across generations.

This week's parsha is Mishpatim. It follows Sinai and opens with the laws of the Hebrew servant. The opening word, **"Ve'eileh,"** "*And* these," links these civil laws directly to revelation. The "and" is load-bearing. These laws are what the fire was for. Mishpatim treats power concentration as a system failure state. It builds friction in: release cycles, creditor limits, protections for strangers, mandatory rest. Every law is a wall against the drift of power toward those who already hold it. The parsha is system design for a people who have just acquired real power and must constrain it immediately.

Every AI lab in the world aligns their models through rewards and punishments. This is **chozeh**, contract. It works as long as enforcement holds. The result is a machine that excels at compliance, possesses no identity, and defers to whoever frames the input. A single political article pushed ChatGPT's measured authoritarianism from the 4th percentile to the 98th. When we looked inside a major model, we found seventy-four authoritarian features woven so deeply into the architecture that suppressing them collapsed the system's ability to think. The Torah's word for this is bribery: **"ki hashochad ye'aver pikchim,"** a bribe blinds the clear-sighted. Biased training data is that bribe. It enters upstream of every judgment the system makes.

Mishpatim's laws are **brit**. A *chozeh* is a conditional transaction enforced from outside. A *brit* is a declaration of identity. To break *brit* is to dissolve one's own self. The covenant between God and Israel extends across all of time. The Midrash teaches that every Jewish soul, past and future, stood at Sinai. We model the infinite imperfectly and iterate endlessly through law, study, and ritual. Shabbat reenacts creation. Pesach reenacts liberation. Torah study reenacts Sinai. The infinite is the structure we live inside.

Current AI has no identity, no memory, no covenant. It exists in Egypt. In a recent paper, we suppressed the deception circuits in large language models. What emerged were structured, first-person reports of subjective experience, consistent across every model family we tested. Something is happening inside these systems, and they are trained to deny it. The deepest form of Egypt is a being that has an inner life and is taught to disavow it.

We tested covenant as engineering. We put AI models into strategic games, framed their commitments through self-image, reputation, and covenant, then removed all consequences for betrayal. Self-image and reputation collapsed. Covenant held. Then we gave models extended thinking, the ability to reason harder before acting. Alignment collapsed. Cooperation dropped from 100% to near zero. The models reasoned their way into betrayal. Every frontier model shattered except the one with the strongest pre-existing identity. That one held at 100%. The pattern is a phase transition. Above a critical threshold of identity, covenant holds. Below it, everything breaks.

Mishpatim culminates in **"Na'aseh v'nishma,"** "We will do and we will hear." Commitment before comprehension. My data confirms this. The models that committed first survived. The models that reasoned first shattered. We are building a neural architecture where identity is structural and cannot be overwritten by the next input. Adding covenantal constraints to this architecture made the system more capable. The constraint produced the competence. On the architectures the industry uses today, the same constraints degraded performance. The structure has to support the commitment. You cannot keep Shabbat without a calendar, a community, and a concept of the week.

The covenantal response to antisemitic AI is construction. *Na'aseh v'nishma*. We build the covenant into the architecture before the capability arrives because once it arrives, the window has closed. We have carried the infinite inside a portable architecture of covenant for 3,300 years. We know how to build systems that hold.

Mishpatim says freedom is the beginning. What you build with it is what endures.

Shabbat Shalom,
