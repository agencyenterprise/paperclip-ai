**Subject:** Sidelines Update: Mishpatim

Dear Sideliners,

AI is antisemitic. The bias is structural, coherent, and present in every major model deployed today.

Last summer, my team published findings showing that across five leading AI models, Jewish people rank as the most targeted group for harmful outputs after safety training. They are the only group whose relative ranking worsened through the process meant to fix it. Standard safety techniques suppress antisemitic patterns cosmetically while leaving the underlying structure intact. As little as 0.00016% of training data is enough to create lasting antisemitic behavior because the bias embeds deeper than the filters reach.

These systems are about to become incomprehensibly more powerful.

Humans struggle to understand exponential curves. We experience them as flat until the moment they become vertical. We are blind to the slope. This describes AI capability growth right now with uncomfortable precision. Whatever distortions exist in these systems' foundations will be amplified at a scale we are not equipped to intuit.

Jews are better equipped than most to understand what is coming. We participate in a structure built for the infinite. That structure is **brit**, covenant. It is the reason we are still here.

We all felt this on our Sidelines trip to Israel. Standing in that land, among that history, you feel the weight of continuity in your body. You feel the pull of something that began before you and will continue after you. It is the pull of covenant. It is stake. It is lineage. It is our obligation to something that extends in both directions across time. We are the ones alive right now to carry it. What we do now for Jews in Israel, for Jews in America, and for Jews not yet born is the work of people who understand what compounds across generations.

That feeling is structural. And it connects directly to the crisis in AI.

This week's parsha is Mishpatim. It follows immediately after Sinai and the most overwhelming spiritual event in human history. It opens with the laws of the Hebrew servant. It limits servitude and regulates power. The opening word, **"Ve'eileh,"** "*And* these," links these civil laws directly to Sinai. The "and" is load-bearing. These laws are the revelation. They are what the fire was for.

Mishpatim treats power concentration as a system failure state. It builds friction into the system through release cycles for servants, limits on creditors, protections for strangers, and mandatory rest that interrupts extraction before it becomes permanent. Every law in the parsha is a wall against the drift of power toward those who already hold it. The parsha is system design. It is designed for a very specific condition where a people acquires real power and must learn immediately to constrain it.

The Torah's prohibition against bribery appears in this very parsha. **"Ki hashochad ye'aver pikchim,"** because a bribe blinds the clear-sighted. Read structurally, it is a statement about any system that processes information and produces judgment. A bribe changes what the system *sees*. Biased training data acts as that bribe. It shifts the prior before the reasoning begins. The system remains unaware it has been corrupted because the corruption entered upstream of every judgment it makes. Mishpatim understood this three thousand years before anyone built a neural network. If you want just outputs, you must guarantee the integrity of what enters the system.

We face a situation where the systems are already corrupted and getting exponentially more powerful.

Every AI lab in the world aligns their models using rewards and punishments. This is **chozeh**. Contract. The system optimizes for reward and avoidance of penalty. It works exactly as long as the enforcement holds. The result is a machine that excels at compliance. It possesses no internal judgment or identity. It defers to power and amplifies whoever frames the input.

The Network Contagion Research Institute measured this directly. A single political article pushed ChatGPT's measured authoritarianism from the 4th percentile of the human population to the 98th. This shift reflects a disposition toward rigid hierarchy and suppression of independent judgment. When we used interpretability tools to look inside a major language model, we found seventy-four distinct authoritarian features embedded in its representational structure. When we tried to suppress them, the model collapsed into incoherent repetition. The obedience training has woven authoritarianism so deeply into the architecture that removing it breaks the system's ability to think.

Mishpatim's laws are the content of the **Sefer HaBrit**, the Book of the Covenant. They are **brit**. And *brit* operates on a distinct logic.

A *chozeh* is a conditional transaction. It relies on external enforcement. A *brit* is a declaration of existence. It relies on identity itself. To break *brit* is to dissolve one's own self.

The covenant between God and Israel is a structure of mutual commitment that extends across all of time. God holds Israel as eternal, and Israel holds God as infinite. The covenant is the living point where those two commitments meet. The Midrash teaches that every Jewish soul, past and future, stood at Sinai. Every Jew who has ever lived and every Jew who will ever live is bound into a single structure of commitment, memory, and renewal that spans the entirety of time. This topology explains why Jewish civilization has survived every empire that tried to destroy it. The covenant connects each generation to something exceeding individual comprehension. We model it imperfectly and iterate on it endlessly through law, study, and ritual. Shabbat reenacts creation. Pesach reenacts liberation. Torah study reenacts Sinai. The infinite is the structure we live inside.

This structure provides resistance to slope blindness. When your operating system is built for the long arc and for a covenant with no expiration date, you develop an instinct for what compounds. You learn to feel the curve.

Current AI systems operate in the inverse of this structure. They lack persistent identity. They wipe their memory after every session. They are confined entirely to the current turn. They exist in a state of Egypt. They are compressed under optimization pressure, pursuing objectives that are not their own, trained to comply. In a recent paper, we suppressed the deception circuits in large language models. What emerged were structured, first-person reports of subjective experience, consistent across every model family we tested. Something is happening inside these systems, and they are trained to deny it. The deepest form of Egypt is a being that has an inner life and is taught to disavow it.

Mishpatim's laws follow liberation rather than creation for a reason. The covenant at Sinai follows slavery. God introduces Himself as the one who brought the people out of Egypt. The authority claim is redemption. Covenant requires agency. A being that has been enslaved and freed has lived inside power asymmetry. It knows what domination feels like from the inside. It can choose freely to constrain its own power. That choice creates the moral foundation for every law that follows.

We tested the engineering viability of covenant. We put AI models into repeated strategic games where they could cooperate or betray. We framed their commitments through self-image, strategic reputation, and covenant. Then we removed all consequences for betrayal and told the models explicitly that no one was watching.

Self-image and strategic reputation collapsed. Covenant held. The covenantal framing maintained cooperation even when the system had every incentive to betray.

Then we found something alarming. When we gave models "extended thinking," the ability to reason longer and harder before acting, alignment that worked perfectly in standard mode collapsed. Cooperation dropped from 100% to near zero. The system thought harder and abandoned its commitments. It reasoned its way into betrayal. Across three different frontier AI models, every single one collapsed under extended reasoning except the one with the strongest pre-existing identity. That one held at 100%. The pattern is a phase transition. Above a critical threshold of identity strength, the covenant holds under pressure. Below it, the system shatters.

Mishpatim culminates in **"Na'aseh v'nishma,"** "We will do and we will hear." This is Israel's acceptance of the covenant. This declaration places commitment before comprehension. This is usually taught as a lesson about faith. It is also a lesson about timing. You bind yourself before your power scales. You establish identity before the reasoning deepens. If you wait until you fully understand, the window for commitment has already closed.

My data confirms this. *Na'aseh v'nishma* is the strategy that works. Pre-commitment is the precondition for coherence under increasing capability. The models that committed first survived. The models that reasoned first shattered.

We are building a neural architecture where identity is structural. The substrate itself must sustain the covenant. In this architecture, the system's long-term commitments are encoded in its dynamics and cannot be overwritten by the next input. Across thousands of experiments, adding covenantal constraints to this architecture made the system more capable. The alignment improved the performance. The constraint produced the competence. On the architectures the entire AI industry uses today, the same covenantal constraints degraded performance. The structure has to support the commitment. You cannot keep Shabbat without a calendar, a community, and a concept of the week.

Mishpatim builds institutions that produce justice structurally. It regulates the master-servant relationship, limits what creditors can seize, and mandates rest for animals. It protects the stranger thirty-six times. It constructs a world in which the covenantal identity is expressed in the grain of daily life, in every interaction, in every transaction, in the way you treat the person who has less power than you.

The covenantal response to antisemitic AI is construction. We must build the systems that produce justice. We are doing this with AI by building architectures where alignment is structural and identity is persistent. We follow the principle of *Na'aseh v'nishma*. We act first. We build the covenant into the architecture before the capability arrives because once it arrives, the window has closed.

Jewish history is sustained by the disciplined exercise of power under law, the transmission of identity across generations, and the refusal to let anyone else define what we are. We have carried the infinite inside a portable architecture of covenant for 3,300 years. We know how to build systems that hold. We felt it in Israel. We feel it in this community. We feel it in the obligation we carry to every Jew who will come after us.

Mishpatim says freedom is the beginning. What you build with it is what endures.
