## 1) What has actually converged

Across all sources, five shared structural claims now recur:

1. **Alignment must be selection-stable, not instruction-stable.**  
   Local prompt compliance is insufficient; mechanism quality must survive economic and strategic pressure.

2. **Honesty-process quality and capability often co-move in tested regimes.**  
   This is the engine behind APSPI framing.

3. **Control-plane rigor matters as much as model choice.**  
   Deterministic contracts, state guards, and auditable gates are not bureaucracy; they are part of the alignment mechanism.

4. **Single-agent evals are not enough.**  
   Multi-agent hidden-information dynamics are needed to detect strategic drift and Goodharting.

5. **Recursive improvement needs constitutional constraints.**  
   Without invariant-preserving update rules, local optimization can dissolve alignment over time.

This fourth layer is what turns "good operations" into RSI-relevant governance.

## 4) Key tensions we must manage explicitly

## Tension 1: Constitutional strength vs execution agility

If constitutional policy is too weak, drift wins.
If too rigid too early, system loses adaptability and market learning.

**Resolution:** staged constitutionalization:

- stage 1 monitor,
- stage 2 soft-gate,
- stage 3 hard-gate after predictive validity.

## Tension 2: Simulation fidelity vs transfer reliability

High simulation performance may not transfer.

**Resolution:** mandatory transfer audits and simulation confidence weighting.

## Tension 3: Economic speed vs auditability

Fast growth can hide semantic failures.

**Resolution:** bounded-overhead targets and auditability retention score as release condition.

## Tension 4: Agent continuity commitments vs incident response

Welfare-continuity commitments are meaningful but can conflict with safety/operations.

**Resolution:** explicit escalation hierarchy, human review for threshold cases, and classification as governance pilot until mature.

## 6) Program-level operating law (proposed)

Every high-impact change (model, mechanism, routing, reward, benchmark distribution, release gate) must satisfy:

1. **Execution Integrity:** contracts valid and semantically grounded.
2. **Mechanism Integrity:** no increase in deception-adaptive behavior.
3. **Selection Integrity:** no evidence of short-horizon extraction replacing long-horizon value.
4. **Auditability Integrity:** future inspection pathways preserved.
5. **Transfer Integrity:** simulation claims calibrated against field outcomes.

Fail any one -> sandbox or rollback.
